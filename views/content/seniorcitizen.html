HEALTH INSURANCE FOR SENIOR CITIZENS

In India, individuals above the age of 60 are considered as senior citizens. Senior citizens usually have limited income or irregular income after their retirement. They are also more susceptible to illnesses and accidents. The combination of these conditions makes it very important to secure their financial future against rising medical costs. Plans that are specifically designed for their needs are Senior Citizen Plans.

WHICH TO BUY SENIOR CITIZEN OR FLOATER?

Family floater plans calculate their premium basis the age of the oldest family member. Besides if they happen to have an existing medical condition, then it unfairly affects the premium rates for the rest of the healthy members. If you are living with dependent parents who are above 60 years then buy senior citizen health insurance plans for them separately instead of including them in a Family Floater plan.

POINTS TO CONSIDER WHILE BUYING SENIOR CITIZEN PLANS

  • Co-payment ratio: Co-payment stipulates that the policyholder will bear a specified percentage of the claim amount. Plans which place a higher share on the policyholder should be avoided.
  • Limit on Surgeries/treatments: Every policy limits certain illnesses or treatments. Generally speaking lesser the limits higher the premium. But the benefits are better when the policy is exercised.
  • Pre-existing diseases: Check the existing diseases covered by the policy. In some policies the policy holder has to wait for few years before making a claim.
  • Waiting Periods: There is a waiting period of 30 days before any claim can be made in most policies. Check if the waiting period is longer.
  • Other Benefits: Some policies don’t cover alternative treatments such as homeopathic, ayurveda etc.

TAX BENEFITS:

Tax deduction is available for Senior Citizen policy up to Rs 30,000 even if one Parent is a senior citizen.
