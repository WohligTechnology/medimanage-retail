Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you really need to know about Health Insurance!

1. What will be covered by a Health Insurance Policy?

A health insurance policy ideally covers 4 categories as mentioned below:

a) In-patient Treatment - Hospitalisation expenses incurred for room rent, boarding expenses, nursing, intensive care unit, medical practitioner, medicines and other related expenses due to an illness or accident.

b) Pre-Hospitalisation- Expenses incurred due to an illness before hospitalisation

c) Post-Hospitalisation- Expenses incurred after discharge from hospitalisation

d) Day care procedures- Treatments which do not require 24 hours hospitalisation

2. Why do I need Health Insurance?

Illness is unforeseen, and cannot be predicted so Health Insurance comes to your rescue and finances your medical expenses.

Moreover the Healthcare expenditure in our country is expensive and so Health Insurance helps in financing the medical emergency.

3. What are the important things to check in the policy?

a) Understand whether the product features/benefits meets not only just your current requirements but also future eventualities.

b) Know the waiting period for pre-existing diseases, usually an insurance company covers the expenses on pre-existing diseases after a period of 4 years.

c) Verify if there is a clause of co-payment (means that the policyholder has to bear some percentage of expense of the claim). The co-pay clause is usually a feature in senior citizen health insurance policies.

d) Check the exclusions in the policy. This is important as one should be aware of the limitations in the policy to avoid chaos at the time of a claim.

e) Check the Proposal form filled & declarations submitted if they have been recorded correctly & completely.

5. What is Pre and Post hospitalization?

Pre- and Post-hospitalization expenses cover all medical expenses incurred within certain number of days prior to hospitalization and expenses incurred within certain number of days post hospitalization provided the expenses were incurred for the same condition for which you were hospitalised

6. What is a waiting period?

A waiting period is the length of time you have to wait before being eligible for claiming Policy benefits. A standard policy would have 3 types of waiting periods:-

In the first year of the policy, there would be a waiting period of 30 days for claims related to sickness.

Any existing ailment for which treatment or any medication is taken would be excluded for minimum period of 4 years. If there are no ailments declared/detected, then there is no waiting period.

For certain specified ailments like Hernia, Cataract, Piles, Knee Replacement, Diabetes, Hypertension the policy has a mandatory waiting period of 1, 2 and 4 years.

7. Why should I take a Health Policy if I already have health insurance from my employer, or if my family and
I are already covered by my corporate?

Your employer will cover your medical expenses only as long as you are employed. Tomorrow, you may change your job, retire, or even start something on your own. In all such cases you and your family will be stuck if a medical emergency arises and you have not arranged for an alternative health insurance policy. It is at this point of time that Health Insurance policy will come to your rescue.

Health Insurance policy can also act as a supplement to your existing medical cover in case the cost of medical treatment is higher than your existing cover level.

8. What is the basic difference between individual and group health insurance coverage?

An individual policy is purchased by you directly from us. In a Group Health Insurance Policy, the employer or legally constituted group is the policyholder and the insurance company contracts with the employer.

9. Is Health insurance Claim payable in Grace period?

The insurance company is not liable to pay for any claim arising out of an Injury/ Accident/ Condition that occurred during the Grace Period. Thus we advise customers to pay the premium atleast 10 days before the due date so that policy can be processed & there is no adverse impact.

10. What is Family Floater?

Family Floater is a policy which covers more than one family member for a fixed cover. Here the fixed cover is shared by all the family members, i.e. if Shah Family of four take a cover of 5 lakhs, the entire family can share the sum insured of Rs. 5 Lakhs during the term of the policy. So, if Mrs. Shah makes a hospitalization claim of Rs. 75000/-, the remaining 4.25 Lakhs can be claimed by the four members in the course of the year.
